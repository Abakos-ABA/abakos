# 13 · Fundraising Playbook: "How do I get the money?"

Honest, prioritized guide. Core idea: **lower capital need first and create proof. Money flows to traction, not plans.**

## Golden rule

Nobody gives a solo founder $1–1.5M for a planning folder alone. Reverse the order:
**first show cheap credibility + first demand → then raise on much better terms.**

---

## Step 0: Shrink the number (most important lever)

You don't need $1.5M immediately. You need a **credible artifact stack** you build almost for free:
- Litepaper + landing page (workstream C): cost: time.
- `btcd` fork that produces blocks locally + reward-split demo (workstream A, trimmed).
- Waitlist/design-partner conversations.

That lowers the first real round to e.g. **$300–500k** (pre-seed through testnet) instead of $1.5M all at once. Stages instead of all-in.

## Step 1: Generate real revenue early (bootstrap + proof)

The strongest fundraising lever in DePIN/AI is **real demand**. You can start this **before** the finished chain:
- **Thin "Abakos Chat" / batch inference reselling** on rented GPUs (RunPod/Vast/neocloud): sell cheap inference/embeddings, in stablecoin.
- That brings: (a) some cash flow, (b) **proof that people pay for cheap compute**, exactly the story investors want.
- Low cost, low risk, and it de-risks the whole protocol narrative.

## Step 2: Win a technical co-founder (biggest multiplier)

Investors bet on **teams**. A solo, non-deep-crypto founder raising an L1 is hard. Bring a **protocol/GPU/crypto engineer** on board (CUDA/ZK/Go). Offer meaningful equity/token (see team bucket 8%). That drastically increases raise odds.

## Step 3: Crypto accelerator (best single move early/solo)

- **Alliance DAO**: strongest crypto-native accelerator: capital + network + demo day. Exactly for early stage. **Apply here first.**
- Others: **Outlier Ventures (Base Camp)**, **a16z CSX**, **Orange DAO**, **Tachyon (Consensys)**, **Y Combinator** (does crypto too).
- Advantage: money **plus** warm intros to VCs **plus** credibility.

## Step 4: Pre-seed from crypto/DePIN VCs + angels

- **Instrument:** SAFT (token-only, decision, see Doc 12 §4a: keeps IOTAplus AG's cap table untouched, no shares issued). Only after legal opinion (Doc 10).
- **Target:** $1–2M (or staged $300–500k first).
- **Relevant funds (DePIN/AI/infra focus):** Multicoin, Delphi Ventures, Hack VC, Dragonfly, Robot Ventures, Maven11, Borderless Capital, Protagonist, Generative Ventures, Escape Velocity, Mechanism, plus DePIN-specific angels.
- **How to get in:** **warm intros > cold outreach.** Build pipeline of 30–50 funds/angels; get introduced via existing founders/accelerator.
- **Build in public on X/Twitter:** show progress regularly → attracts **inbound** from investors and co-founders.

## Step 5: Strategic neocloud partner (capital + supply)

A GPU provider (Together/io.net/Vast/RunPod-like) as **strategic investor** solves two problems at once: money **and** supply side (GPUs in network, Doc 06). Very attractive deal type.

## Step 6: Grants (parallel, "free" money)

- Ecosystem/DePIN grants, research grants (we build on open cuPOW paper), Gitcoin/quadratic funding.
- Small but non-dilutive; run in parallel.

## Step 7: Later: community/private round & launchpad

Only **after** product/testnet and audience: small, vested SAFT or launchpad round near mainnet (phase 3). Never as first step (credibility).

## Reality check

- Crypto fundraising 2026 is **selective**; DePIN/PoUW is hot **but crowded** (Pearl, Gonka, Titan, io.net). Differentiation (enforced utility + consumer chat) + **demo + co-founder + first revenue** is what makes a round realistic.
- If VC doesn't land: **bootstrap via inference revenue** + grants until testnet/audience, then community round. The project is built so it can start without a large round.
